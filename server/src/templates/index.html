Open Reading Frames (ORF)

Welcome. Please try my ORF finder!

In molecular biology, open reading frames (ORFs) are defined as spans of DNA sequence between the start and stop codons. Usually, this is considered within a studied region of a prokaryotic DNA sequence, where only one of the six possible reading frames will be 'open' (the 'reading', however, refers to the RNA produced by transcription of the DNA and its subsequent interaction with the ribosome in translation). Such an ORF may contain a start codon (usually ATG in terms of RNA) and by definition cannot extend beyond a stop codon (usually TGA, TAG TAA)
